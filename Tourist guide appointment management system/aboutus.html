ABOUT JASAAN
Jasaan was named by a Spanish missionary who chanced upon a native man (possibly from the Higaonon tribe) sharpening his bolo in Sapong Spring in what is now called Kota, Aplaya. The missionary asked for the name of the place; however, the native replied, "Ag-hasaa," meaning "I am sharpening my bolo." Jasaan is politically subdivided into 15 barangays, each barangay consisting of puroks, while some have sitios. There are 8 barangays which are considered coastal barangays (Aplaya, Solana, Luz Banzon, Kimaya, Lower Jasaan, Bobuntugan, Jampason, and San Antonio) and 7 inland barangays (Upper Jasaan, Corrales, San Isidro, Natubo, Danao, San Nicolas, and Ignacio S. Cruz). Enjoy the best activities in Jasaan, Philippines, such as Agutaya Island, Sagpulon Waterfall, Twin Heart Resort, Spring View Resort, Liang Beach, Immaculate Conception Church, and Liang Rock.
